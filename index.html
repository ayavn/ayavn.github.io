The phone is terrible for cloud gaming

Updated: Wed 06 July 2022
By Hoang Trong Anh

Valve‚Äôs enormous do-anything handheld PC has made me realize that, actually, sometimes dedicated gaming hardware is good! I still needed my phone to have power the rest of the trip.

Cloud gaming also interferes with all the other stuff phones are good for. Notifications from other non-gaming apps make themselves known at the most irritating of times. Most services include an overlay of touchscreen controls. I‚Äôd probably just rather have a whole separate device.

After my Steam Deck arrived, I was forced to reckon with my affection, but also severe dissatisfaction, with cloud gaming on the phone. For the last two weeks, I‚Äôve been playing Star Wars Jedi: Fallen Order on Xbox Cloud Gaming on my Steam Deck, and besides feeling the same sense of wonder as when I first cloud gamed on my phone, I also get the bonus of actually liking the experience.

This is a way to cloud game, but it is not the best way to cloud game.Instead of being a last resort, cloud gaming feels like a first choice when I‚Äôm doing it on the Steam Deck. I can‚Äôt wait to get Sony and Nvidia and even Google‚Äôs cloud gaming solutions up and running on the thing. The game doesn‚Äôt feel crowded. The controls just work. Do I want something lighter and quieter than the Steam Deck for all my cloud gaming needs? Sure. Phones can already do the work, and with new beefy mobile GPUs on the horizon like ARM‚Äôs ray-tracing-capable Immortalis, the possibility of having a really good mobile gaming solution without the need for custom chips (which both the Nintendo Switch and Steam Deck rely on), feels closer than ever. Companies like Ayn and GPD are trying to build them. But, if the goal is to just cloud game on the go, I don‚Äôt need an Immortalis GPU. When the internet is good, cloud gaming is more than enough for AAA gaming on the go. Which means you don‚Äôt need the most powerful GPU or CPU.
